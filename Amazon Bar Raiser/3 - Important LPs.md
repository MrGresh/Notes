## The five most frequently tested and heavily weighted LPs in a Bar Raiser interview include:

### 1. Customer Obsession

#### What they look for: 
Leaders start with the customer and work backwards. They vigorously work to earn and keep customer trust.
#### Bar Raiser Focus: 
They test whether you make decisions based on long-term customer value rather than short-term gains, and whether you use direct customer feedback to guide your projects.


### 2. Ownership

#### What they look for: 
Leaders are long-term thinkers who act on behalf of the entire company, beyond just their own team.
#### Bar Raiser Focus: 
They assess if you take personal responsibility for outcomes (both successes and failures) and if you consistently go above and beyond your standard job description to ensure a project succeeds.


### 3. Deliver Results

#### What they look for: 
Leaders focus on the key inputs for their business and deliver them with the right quality and in a timely manner.
#### Bar Raiser Focus: 
They look for proof of execution, resilience through obstacles, and a strong track record of actually launching or completing what you set out to do.


### 4. Earn Trust

#### What they look for: 
Leaders listen attentively, speak candidly, and treat others respectfully. They are vocally self-critical and treat people with respect.
#### Bar Raiser Focus: 
They evaluate your ability to admit mistakes, take accountability, and build consensus or psychological safety within cross-functional teams.


### 5. Dive Deep

#### What they look for: 
Leaders operate at all levels, stay connected to the details, audit frequently, and are skeptical when metrics and anecdote differ.
#### Bar Raiser Focus: 
They want to see that you intimately know the data and mechanics of your work, rather than just understanding high-level summaries.


## 💡 The "Bar Raiser" Wildcard Principles

While the five LPs above drive the bulk of the assessment, a Bar Raiser is also specifically tasked with assessing three cultural fit principles to ensure you elevate the team:

### Have Backbone; Disagree and Commit: 
Can you respectfully challenge leadership or the status quo when you disagree, but fully commit once a decision is made?

### Hire and Develop the Best: 
Do you elevate those around you, mentor junior staff, and raise the standard of the people you work with?

### Invent and Simplify: 
Do you find innovative ways to solve complex problems and eliminate redundancies?
